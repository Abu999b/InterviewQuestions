Personality

1. What are your hobbies and interests?

I enjoy coding outside of academics, especially building side projects or contributing to open-source.

I like reading tech blogs and articles about AI, machine learning, and cloud computing.

I’m also interested in problem-solving platforms like Codeforces and LeetCode.

In my free time, I love gaming, especially strategy and puzzle games.

I also enjoy playing chess, which sharpens my critical thinking.

2. How would your friends describe you?

My friends would describe me as a reliable and dedicated individual.

I am always ready to help others, whether with coding problems or advice.

They appreciate my ability to stay calm under pressure and focus on solutions.

They would also say I’m a good communicator and enjoy sharing knowledge.

Overall, I believe they see me as a hard-working and fun-loving person.

3. What values are important to you in a job?

I value an environment that encourages learning and innovation.

A company with a strong mentorship culture is important for my growth.

I appreciate transparency, collaboration, and mutual respect within teams.

Work-life balance and opportunities for personal development are key factors.

Lastly, I seek a role where I can contribute meaningfully and make an impact.

4. Are you willing to relocate or travel for work?

Yes, I am open to relocation if it provides the right growth opportunities.

I am flexible and understand that moving can open new professional avenues.

Traveling for work is also something I’d be comfortable with, especially if it enhances my skills.

I see relocation or travel as a way to experience different work cultures and expand my horizons.

I’m adaptable and willing to step outside my comfort zone for professional development.

5. Do you prefer working alone or in a team? Why?

I prefer working in a team as it fosters collaboration and diverse problem-solving approaches.

I enjoy sharing ideas and learning from others while contributing my expertise.

However, I can also work independently when necessary, especially in tasks that require focus.

I think both team and individual work complement each other in a professional setting.

Ultimately, I enjoy the balance of both, depending on the task at hand.
